I design and develop for the web. I mostly write Vue, React and TypeScript, and have been spending time on the backend side lately — APIs, data, the parts that make the UI actually do something. Front-end is still where I'm sharpest, but I like seeing how the whole thing fits together. I'm always picking something up. The tools change. The curiosity doesn't.

Lately I've been into open source and poking at AI tools. Writing ideas down in my notes — half-formed ideas I'm slowly turning into things. Currently maintaining the [Filipino Food API](https://github.com/eigdoyr/filipino-food-api) and I'm building [Mockymax](https://github.com/eigdoyr/mockymax) — a free device mockup tool for web design showcases.

Before tech, I spent a decade in customer experience across major American publications — [Tribune Publishing](https://www.tribpub.com/), [Los Angeles Times](https://www.latimes.com/), and a few others — until the pivot. Front-end at [MC2](https://mc-2.com/), then visual design for AI training models at [Wirestock](https://wirestock.io/). Different worlds, same attention to how things work for real people.
